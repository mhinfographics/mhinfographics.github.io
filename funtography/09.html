The Land of Brits’ Disappointment

While surfing the web, I stumbled with this place called Disappointment Island. At first glance, I thought wasn't real, but actually is a thing you can put type and find in google maps!
This tiny human-uninhabited island in the subantarctic Auckland Islands of New Zealand was just perfect for a hand-drawn map, I thought immediately.

If the images that came out of google are accurate, this place is not dissapointing at all, or at least not in the summer. Accoringly to Wiki, on 1907, the Dundoland shipwrecked near the west side of the island and the surviving crew was stranded there for seven months. However, its european name, ´Disappointment´ Wiki says, was given to it by another british sailor who was there conducting a survey of fur seals. –Spoiler– He didn't find any, so He named the island after the results of his expedition. –Dissapointing Island.
