Happy Birthday Costa Rica!

This year Costa Rica turns 204 years of independence. My country is a little piece of land of about 51,100 square kilometers. However the seas sorounding it, make it a bit larger, about 589,600 square kilometers of maritime territory. That's about 11.5 times the size of the land area.
While browsing around, I came across this map showing tectonic plates and elevation profiles, and I thought it would be fun to make a 'terraced' version... so, here it is.

There has always been a great appreciation for nature conservation in Costa Rica, in fact, the country expanded the protected areas around the Cocos Island a few years ago. The move aims to protect this crucial corridor for many species in this region of the Pacific Ocean.

If you're curious about the source of the information, I got the contour profiles from Axismaps, and the rest is just Natural Earth and the original map I described above. I used QGIS to generate the base map, Blender to render the terrain and Adibe Illustrator for labels. Thanks for visiting my blog, and happy birthday, Costa Rica!.
