Myanmar political violence

Myanmar is home to about 57 million. The British ruled the country (Burma) since 1820 and gained full independence in 1948, but since then civil wars have asphyxiated the country mostly due ethical conflicts and the struggle to balance power and the militias.

The map above visualizes reports of political violence in Myanmar this year from both, state forces and political militias clustered in a 10km-grid. The details below show areas with high activity, red for state forces and blue for militias.

Since 2010, ACLED has been documenting political violence in Myanmar; you can find out more about their reports and how they gather information about this country and other conflicts on their website.
