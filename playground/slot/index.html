Randomness

The art of uncertainty

Randomness is key in many aspects of our daily lives. Cryptography uses "randomness" to generate secure keys and guarantee the unpredictability of encrypted data, although with increasingly powerful computers these methods may soon become obsolete.

Nature also plays with randomness sometimes. I read at Scientific American that radioactivity decays randomly, isn’t that amazingly interesting? They say that atoms decay randomly at any given moment in time. Even tho that’s an article for kids, it’s quite interesting to read... Or I might enjoy too much kids’ books, that’s also a possibility. The point is randomness is present in many ways in our lives. Or maybe a sort of randomness or chaos like in games.

Slot machines

I thought of giving this computer a task, I wrote a little script to make the images below line up, the code starts at a "random" position, then moves each line to 12 random positions and repeats the movement until all three lines are aligned. It’s kind of satisfying to watch a machine struggle to complete a task, it’s also super silly to see that sometimes it just sits there... and moves wrong.

For sure an AI could do it easily, but my point is that this silly code can show you that sometimes this task is solved in a just a few attempts, other times with much more difficulty. I ran this script a few times and the quickest it took was 6 spins. I wonder how many spins were for you?

If we go technical, that example is not really random because you can trace back its logic. If you are really really unlucky, you will see the spiners do 1.728 attempts to line up the images. But here’s when things get interesting, you might have the sense that the spiners would align in the next spin, because they go so close to the right combination, but they don’t, that’s why people go to try their luck at casinos. With this example, you have about 14% of chance to get the right combination in less than 300 attempts, but you also have 86% of chance to not get it.

That’s how casinos make money, they have higher chances to win than you do. In this case 9 to 1... That’s also the art of uncertainty used to clear up your pockets.
